Math

Math rounds consist of individual rounds, team rounds, and tiebreakers. Computational devices, graph paper, and protractors are prohibited. Rulers and compasses are allowed.

For all tests, no computational devices, including but not limited to calculators, electronic devices, or abaci, are allowed. Similarly, graph paper and protractors are prohibited, but rulers and compasses are permitted.

These tests will be conducted over Zoom. Throughout the entire duration of the contest, you must have your video on. At the beginning of the contest, rename yourself to put your individual ID in front of your name.

For the individual, team, and tiebreaker rounds, you will receive a PDF containing the problems at the start of your allotted time. A timer will be on the screen reminding you of how much time is left, and you must submit the answer form within 1 minute of the timer expiring. If you have any clarification questions about the problems, you can directly message the proctors over Zoom, and they will get back to you with an answer.

Individual Round

The Individual Round contains 10 problems in 60 minutes. It will be split into two divisions, middle school and high school. High school students must take the high school division tests, while middle school students have a choice between middle school and high school.

All answers will be integers.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: How can I prepare for the individual round?

A: Since our problems are designed to prepare you for contests while promoting creative thinking, taking previous AMC 8s and AMC 10/12s can help you prepare for this contest.

Q: What is the difficulty of the individual rounds?

A: For the middle school division, the difficulty of the questions steadily increases from mid AMC 8 to early AIME, while for the high school division, the difficulty increases from AMC 10/12 - late AIME

Q: How many individuals will get awards?

A: The top 5 scorers in the middle school and high school divisions will receive prizes.

Team Round

The Team Round contains 15 problems in 60 minutes. Each team will have up to 4 members. Each team member must be in the same division (middle school or high school).

During the contest, each team will be allotted their own breakout room, where they can talk amongst themselves. Proctors will regularly check in on the breakout rooms. For clarification questions, you may come to the main room to ask a proctor, and they will send a global message if the question can be answered.

The total score of a team is the average score of the individuals, plus the score of the team round. Top 5 teams in each division will earn prizes.

If you have a team of less than 4 members, you may indicate on the registration form for us to merge you with another team. At the registration deadline, any teams that have been merged will be notified about their new IDs. You will only be merged with teams in the same division as you.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: What is the difficulty of the team rounds?

A: The problems will range from mid AMC 10 to early USAMO level. Do not be alarmed if you struggle on some of these problems, they are meant to challenge even the best students.

Q: How can we prepare for the team round?

A: You should get to know your team well—your team’s strengths and weaknesses. Split up the problems you are confident you can solve, and collaborate on the harder ones.

Q: Am I at a disadvantage if I have fewer people on my team?

A: Unfortunately, yes. However, if you so choose, you can combine your team with others, and we will do our best to match you with another willing team in the same division.

Tiebreaker Round

Anyone who ties for a top 10 score in each individual division will participate in a Tiebreaker Round. The Tiebreaker test has 5 problems that will challenge even the strongest competitors; the problems range from mid-AIME difficulty to Olympiad difficulty. The Tiebreaker Round proceeds as follows: questions are shown one at a time, with the most challenging question shown first, with the following time limits:

Question 1 - 25 minutes

Question 2 - 15 minutes

Question 3 - 10 minutes

Question 4 - 5 minutes

Question 5 - 5 minutes

Within the time limit, you may submit to the same question multiple times; however, ONLY your last submission, and the time it took for the last submission, will count.

The first contestant to solve the first question wins the Tiebreaker Round; other contestants who solve the first question will be ranked by speed. Any contestants who submit an incorrect answer move on to the second question, which will break ties by the same process. This process repeats until all ties are broken. In the final round, if more than two people answer incorrectly, the person who answers incorrectly first places higher. All participants in the tiebreaker round must stay for the whole round in order to not reveal the final rankings.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: If I solve the first question, should I still participate in the rest of the Tiebreaker Round?

A: Yes. We will not reveal correct answers to the tiebreaker problems until the end of the Tiebreaker Round. This way, participants can have fun solving all the problems, whether they are already ranked or not.

Q: How would we know who is invited to the Tiebreaker Round?

A: We will announce who is invited to the Tiebreaker Round on the Discord.

Q: What should you do if you are not invited to the Tiebreaker Round?

A: You can participate in other fun activities like the Estimathon.

Countdown Round

After tiebreakers, the top 8 scorers in each division will participate in the Countdown Round. Contestants will be seeded by their rankings in the individual round, with ties broken by the Tiebreaker Round. Brackets will not be reseeded; that is, the bracket determined by the seeding at the start will be the final bracket that is played.

The Countdown Round consists of short, easy problems that last 45 seconds each. As soon as the allotted time begins, the problem will be shown on the screen. A reader will then read the problem out loud. Participants are allowed to buzz in the middle of reading the question.

In every round, two players will compete to answer the question as quickly and accurately as possible; the first person to answer correctly gains a point. A player must say their answer within 3 seconds of buzzing; attempting to stall by using filler words or restating the question will automatically disqualify you from answering the question. Players may not answer a question again after answering incorrectly. No points are awarded if no person answers the question correctly when the time runs out. In all rounds except the final, the first person to receive 2 points moves on. In the final round, the first person to receive 3 points will win the Countdown Round. The winner in each division will earn an additional prize.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: If we do not qualify for the Countdown Round, can we still watch it?

A: Yes. In fact, we encourage students to not only watch, but also play along with the contestants, to improve their skills and learn from the top contestants.

Integration Bee

Each question on the Integration Bee consists of a single integral, of which you must find a numerical answer (in the case of a definite integral), or an expression (in the case of an indefinite integral). The Integration Bee consists of two rounds: a Qualification Round, and a Knockout Round.

The Qualification Round of the Integration Bee will consist of 10 integrals. You will have two minutes to solve each integral. The top 8 scoring participants from the Qualification Round will then compete in the knockout round (with ties being broken by total time taken).

The Knockout Round of the Integration Bee will be a single elimination tournament style bracket. In a matchup, the two players are given the same integral and 2 minutes to solve it. If a player gets it wrong, they will not be allowed to answer until the other player answers incorrectly. The winner after the first three integrals will advance; if it remains a tie after 3 integrals, players will keep attempting integrals until one wins. In the final, the winner after the first five integrals wins, instead of the first three.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: If I qualify for the Tiebreaker Round, can I still participate?

A: No. Unfortunately, it is not possible for you to participate in this event if you were a part of the tiebreaker round, although you may still observe the Knockout Rounds after you are done with the Tiebreaker.

Estimathon

Estimathons are completed by individuals or teams, with teams up to four people. The team that gets the highest score in the Estimathon will earn prizes.

In an estimathon, you are trying to answer each problem to the best of your ability. To answer, you must enter a pair of positive integers (a, b), such that a < b. Submissions that do not meet these requirements will not be considered, and you will receive 0 points for that problem. Your score will be calculated as follows: if the correct answer A satisfies A ∈ [a, b), then you will receive ⌊100 a/(b-1)⌋ points for the problem. Otherwise, you will receive 0 points for that problem. (Thus, the maximum possible score you can receive for each problem is 100)

You must have your camera on at all times, and using Google or any calculators will result in an automatic disqualification.

Physics

The physics round consists of one test. Computational devices are prohibited.

For all tests, no computational devices, including but not limited to calculators, electronic devices, or abaci, are allowed.

This test will be conducted over Zoom. Throughout the entire duration of the contest, you must have your video on. At the beginning of the contest, rename yourself to put your individual ID in front of your name.

During the contest, each team will be allotted their own breakout room, where they can talk amongst themselves. Proctors will regularly check in on the breakout rooms. For clarification questions, you may come to the main room to ask a proctor, and they will send a global message if the question can be answered. You will receive a PDF containing the problems at the start of your allotted time.

The score for the team round will be the total number of points earned from each question.

The Physics Test

The Physics Test contains 25 problems in 180 minutes (3 hours). Each team will have up to 4 members.

Each question is weighted according to its relative difficulty (weights are subjective and have no indication of the problem’s true difficulty - what we find hard for us may not be hard for you). Partial credit will be awarded (show your work).

Conventions

All questions require symbolic expressions. Ignore relativistic or quantum effects unless the problem states otherwise. Leave all trigonometric functions in your answer like tan(2) as is. Leave any operators such as max(x,y), abs(x), floor(x) as is unless they can be further simplified.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: What topics are covered on this test?

A: Topics that may appear include everything on the IPhO syllabus (mechanics, electricity & magnetism, thermodynamics, waves, and modern physics). Not all topics are necessarily covered.

It is recommended to have a solid understanding of calculus and geometry. Some questions may be mathematically more intense than others

Q: How difficult is this test?

A: Questions on this test range from difficulties of a standard AP Physics test to International Olympiad levels (IPhO, IZhO, …). Do not be alarmed if you are unable to make progress on a portion of the questions.

Q: What materials should I prepare for the competition?

A: Be prepared to use up to 15-20 sheets of scratch paper, although depending on the student, not all of it may be necessary. Rulers and compasses are permitted and may be useful.

Once again, all computational aids, such as calculators, abaci, and electronic devices, are prohibited on the test.

Programming

The programming competition will consist of 12 questions, to be completed in 3 hours.

For the programming competition, you may compete in teams of up to four individuals. The programming competition will be held on Codeforces . Each team should sign up with one account, which every member of the team should be able to access. Register here to create an account on Codeforces. Once you have registered an account on Codeforces, use that account to request to become a Participant in this Group. Remember that ONLY the account that you registered with will be admitted into the group. Before the start of the contest, a contest manager will admit you into the group, where you will then be able to take the contest.

Any outside sources are prohibited during the contest, although code that has been saved on your computer beforehand is permitted.

Teams will be ranked by the total number of points they get. Teams can get up to 100 points on each problem, with points given to subtasks based on the problem description. Ties will be broken by the time of the last submission that added points to the team’s overall score. Any clarification questions can be raised within the Codeforces contest.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: What is the difficulty of the problems?

A: There are a range of difficulties to the problems. It ranges from easy Bronze problems to hard Platinum level problems. Don’t be afraid if you can’t solve some of the later problems, as they are designed to take time to solve.

Q: What languages are supported?

A: Codeforces supports a variety of languages, such as python, java, and C++.

Q: Will different languages get different time limits?

A: No. Slower languages will not be afforded extra time.

General FAQs

Q: Can I register for multiple different subjects?

A: Yes. You can register for whichever subjects you want to take. We recommend that you register for all of them.

Q: How do I register for multiple subjects?

A: You can fill out the registration form multiple times, each time registering with a different subject.

Q: Can I have different teams for different subjects?

A: Yes. You may have different team members for different subjects. While we will announce which individuals had the highest overall scores at the end of the competitions, we will not give separate prizes for it.
