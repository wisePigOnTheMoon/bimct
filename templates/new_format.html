Math

Math rounds consist of individual rounds, team rounds, and tiebreakers. Computational devices, graph paper, and protractors are prohibited. Rulers and compasses are allowed.

For all tests, no computational devices, including but not limited to calculators, electronic devices, or abaci, are allowed. Similarly, graph paper and protractors are prohibited, but rulers and compasses are permitted. These tests will be conducted over Zoom. Throughout the entire duration of the contest, you must have your video on. At the beginning of the contest, rename yourself to put your individual ID in front of your name. For the individual, team, and tiebreaker rounds, you will receive a PDF containing the problems at the start of your allotted time. A timer will be on the screen reminding you of how much time is left, and you must submit the answer form within 1 minute of the timer expiring. If you have any clarification questions about the problems, you can directly message the proctors over Zoom, and they will get back to you with an answer.

Physics

The physics round consists of one test. Computational devices are prohibited.

For all tests, no computational devices, including but not limited to calculators, electronic devices, or abaci, are allowed. This test will be conducted over Zoom. Throughout the entire duration of the contest, you must have your video on. At the beginning of the contest, rename yourself to put your individual ID in front of your name. During the contest, each team will be allotted their own breakout room, where they can talk amongst themselves. Proctors will regularly check in on the breakout rooms. For clarification questions, you may come to the main room to ask a proctor, and they will send a global message if the question can be answered. You will receive a PDF containing the problems at the start of your allotted time. The score for the team round will be the total number of points earned from each question.

Programming

The programming competition will consist of 12 questions, to be completed in 3 hours.

For the programming competition, you may compete in teams of up to four individuals. The programming competition will be held on https://codeforces.com. Each team should sign up with one account, which every member of the team should be able to access. You can use https://codeforces.com/register to register an account on Codeforces. Once you have registered an account on Codeforces, use that account to request to become a Participant in the following group: https://codeforces.com/group/Am7lqUxQS0. Remember that ONLY the account that you registered with will be admitted into the group. Before the start of the contest, a contest manager will admit you into the group, where you will then be able to take the contest. Any outside sources are prohibited during the contest, although code that has been saved on your computer beforehand is permitted. Teams will be ranked by the total number of points they get. Teams can get up to 100 points on each problem, with points given to subtasks based on the problem description.
