Testing Information

Individual Round

The Individual Round is 10 problems and 60 minutes. The middle school test will contain problems ranging from AMC 8 to mid/late AMC 10, while the high school test will contain problems ranging from AMC 10 to mid/late AIME. Each Individual Round question is worth the same amount of points.

Team Round

The Team Round is 15 problems, lasts 60 minutes, and will be completed by teams of at most 6. The test will contain problems harder than the Individual Round, from Mid AMC 10 difficulty to late AIME difficulty. Each problem is worth the same amount of points.

Tiebreaker Round

Anyone who ties for a top 10 score in each division will participate in a Tiebreaker Round. The Tiebreaker test has 5 problems that will challenge even the strongest competitors; the problems range from mid-AIME difficulty to Olympiad difficulty. The Tiebreaker Round proceeds as follows: questions are shown one at a time, with the most challenging question shown first. The times for the questions are as follows: Question 1, 25 minutes; Question 2, 15 minutes; Question 3, 10 minutes; Question 4, 5 minutes; Question 5, 5 minutes. The first contestant to solve the first question wins the Tiebreaker Round; other contestants who solve the first question will be ranked by speed. Any contestants who submit an incorrect answer move on to the second question; similarly, accuracy and speed determine the rankings. This process repeats until all ties are broken. In the final round, if more than two people answer incorrectly, the person to answer incorrectly first places higher. All participants in the tiebreaker round must stay for the whole round in order to not betray the final rankings.

Countdown Round

After tiebreakers, the top 8 scorers in each division will participate in the Countdown Round. The Countdown Round consists of short, easy problems that last 45 seconds each. In every round, two players will compete to answer the question as quickly and accurately as possible; the first person to answer correctly gains a point. Players may not answer a question again after answering incorrectly. No points are awarded if no person answers the question correctly when the time runs out. In all rounds except the final, the first person to receive 2 points moves on. In the final round, the first person to receive 3 points will win the Countdown Round.

Rules and Format

Contest Rules and Format:

-For all tests, no computational devices, including but not limited to calculators, electronic devices, or abaci, are allowed. Similarly, graph paper and protractors are prohibited, but rulers and compasses are permitted.

-The BIMCT takes academic honesty seriously. Any incidences of cheating will result in an immediate disqualification and a possible ban from future contests.

-All answers except in the Countdown Round and Post-Contest games will be integer answers.

-The entire exam will be conducted online over Zoom with a proctor in each Zoom room. Join our discord or check out our website to view the Zoom link when it is posted.

-All answers will be submitted through Google Forms.

-The Google Forms and Problem Set PDFs will be sent to the Zoom room on the contest day.

-Students can submit problem disputes through a form on the contest day if they disagree with an answer. The deadline for problem disputes will similarly be announced.

Scoring

- Individual Scores will be determined solely by the number of questions correctly answered on the Individual Round.

- Ties will be broken through the Tiebreaker Round as described before. The maximum Individual Score is 10.

- The Team Score is determined by the number of questions correctly answered on the Team Round. Teams will be ranked based on the following formula:

- (Sum of Individual Scores of all team members / Number of people on the team) * 6 + (10 * Team Round Score)

- The maximum Team Score is 210.
